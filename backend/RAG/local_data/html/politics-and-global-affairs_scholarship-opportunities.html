Scholarship Opportunities

Scholarships not only help you out financially, but also allow you to showcase your hard work and dedication on your resume and future application materials. Moreover, the process of applying for a scholarship will help you work on your writing, networking, and organizational skills. The more scholarships you apply to, the more chances you have of receiving one! So don't wait, research available scholarships, prepare your application materials early, and apply! 

We've compiled a list of some of the most popular PSU scholarships our students have applied to below. Click here to go to the PSU Scholarships and PSU Fellowships websites for the complete database of opportunities available through PSU. 

Oregon Consular Corps International Studies Scholarship

Each year, the Oregon Consular Corps awards $3000 to two PSU undergraduate students majoring in International & Global Studies or Political Science with an interest in international affairs. 

Consul members of the Oregon Consular Corps (OCC) are committed to improved relations, communications, understanding, and trade between nations, peoples, and regions of the world. Oregon public universities are poorly funded and fewer young people in Oregon have the opportunity to pursue their educational and career dreams. Oregon Consular Corps members remember their own educational dreams and objectives and want more students in Oregon to have the opportunity to build a better world and better lives for themselves and for others.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Must be US citizen, or legal resident, or international student on student visa
  • Completed a minimum of 12 credit hours INTL or PS coursework focused on international issues
  • Declared Political Science or International & Global Studies major as of Fall 2024
  • Junior class standing or above (90+ credits) in good academic standing
  • Maintain full-time status (12 credits per term) during the 2024-2025 academic year (fall, winter, and spring)
  • Maintain a cumulative GPA of 3.00
  • Must demonstrate or possess a second language proficiency, equivalent to that attained at the end of the second year of studies
  • Note: past recipients of the scholarship are not eligible to apply again

What You Need to Apply:

  • Download and fill out the OCC application form
  • A current resume/CV
  • A copy of your unofficial PSU transcript
  • Two letters of reference: At least one letter should be from a PSU faculty member or advisor with specific knowledge of your international study, international-related work experience, volunteer experience, or school work. Please ask for your reference letters to be emailed directly to us at pga@pdx.edu by the October 25 deadline.

Application form, resume/CV, and unofficial PSU transcript must be emailed to pga@pdx.edu by the deadline below. Please send all three attachments in one email. 

2024-25 Application Deadline is October 25, 2024
Finalists will be invited for an interview in early December.
Award recipients will be announced mid-December.
Scholarships will be awarded for Winter term.

Benjamin A. Gilman International Scholarship Program

The U.S. Department of State’s Benjamin A. Gilman International Scholarship Program (Gilman Program) enables students of limited financial means to study or intern abroad, providing them with skills critical to our national security and economic prosperity. To be eligible for the Gilman Program, applicants must be receiving a Federal Pell Grant during the time of application or provide proof that they will be receiving a Pell Grant during the term of their study abroad program or internship.

 

Institute for Asian Studies Scholarship

Purpose: The Institute for Asian Studies Student Scholarship is a competitive award of up to $1000 for one or two qualified, full-time, degree seeking, PSU students, who are pursuing an academic program at Portland State, or abroad, with a focus on Asia and/or Asian language studies.
Amount: Up to $1000
Criteria for Selection: 

  • Be a U.S. citizen or permanent resident
  • Be  full-time enrolled or accepted for full-time enrollment at Portland State University, as a degree-seeking undergraduate or graduate student.
  • Be pursuing an academic program at PSU, or abroad, with a focus on Asia and/or Asian language studies.

Apply through PSU Scholarship Application
Criteria for Selection: 

  • Open to PSU students who have formally studied Arabic for at least two years.
  • Must have a cumulative GPA of 3.4 or higher.
  • Commitment to successfully complete the Minor in Arabic, or the Major in Arabic Language & Literature at PSU, and/or Certificate in Middle Eastern Studies.
  • To qualify for this scholarship MESC Students must take Ar 301-2-3 or Ar 304-5-6 or higher level Arabic classes).
  • Academic excellence and financial need are a major consideration.

Matching Fulbright Grants

Fulbright scholars who are admitted and enroll in Portland State University degree programs will receive a matching grant of up to $4,500 per year. This renewable matching scholarship is available to all Fulbright Scholars who begin a degree program at Portland State University. The grant will match up to $4,500 of Fulbright sponsorship during each academic year in which admitted Fulbright scholars are enrolled in a Portland State degree program.
