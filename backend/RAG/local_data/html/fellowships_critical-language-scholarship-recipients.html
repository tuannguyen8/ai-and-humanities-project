Critical Language Scholarship Recipients

Meet PSU's CLS Recipients

Summer 2025

Jean-Aime Polneau - Turkey

Before enrolling at PSU, Jean-Aime Polneau was attending Bushnell University in Eugene, Oregon, where he had a scholarship to play soccer. Unfortunately, due to an injury he later ended up at PSU. 

Around April 2024, during one of his work shifts, Jean-Aime had a conversation with a medicine delivery driver who was retired from his career as an accountant in the public sector. The two talked about various career options that were available and also about the value of studying languages. this lead Jean-Aime to investigate further. He found a video about Turkish language and listened to what it sounded like. He liked what he heard so much that, in that moment, he made the decision to study Turkish. 

Jean-Aime is also enrolled in the Russian Flagship Program (RFP) and through this program he saw an email about the Criticial Language Scholarship (CLS). This is when he learned that CLS offers more than just a Russian study abroad scholarship. He saw that Turkish was also a critical language, along with several others, and that's when he knew instantly he had to apply. He is now very excited to try authentic Turkish cuisine, have opportunities to speak and practice Turkish daily, and to meet and make friends in another part of the world. 

Summer 2024

Macie Harreld - Russian

Macie has a B.A. in Spanish and is a post-bac student at PSU, studying English, linguistics, and Russian. During her undergraduate studies, she was able to volunteer as an English tutor for Spanish speakers, and fell in love with language teaching. After graduation, she began working as an English teacher in St. Petersburg, Russia, where she started learning Russian. She is now in her second year of PSU’s Russian Flagship Program, and is tremendously excited and grateful for the opportunity to continue studying Russian this summer in Tbilisi, Georgia on the CLS program. She intends to pursue a M.A. in TESOL, and eventually use her knowledge of foreign languages and cultures in her career as a language educator.

Summer 2023

Quinn Bicer - Arabic

Quinn Bicer is a junior at PSU in the University Honors College, graduating in Spring 2024. He is working towards graduating with majors in Middle East studies and Arabic, a minor in applied linguistics, and certificates in TESL, Arabic language pedagogy, and intercultural competence for the workplace. Quinn is currently a third year Arabic student, tutors Arabic at the Learning Center, and leads the Arabic Club at PSU.

Before entering PSU, Quinn taught theatre in India for five and a half months, where he discovered his love for language, teaching, and exploring other cultures, but he did not start seriously learning Arabic until he came to PSU, and he developed a passion for the Arabic language through his classes in the Arabic department.

Quinn desires to work in education and research in the future, and his particular interests include folklore, language pedagogy, and world religions and cultures. He is fascinated by how humans create and share meaning in their lives and communities. Outside of school, Quinn enjoys music, reading, art, stage manages theatre productions, and is involved in the local performance scene.

Quinn is thrilled and honored to be going to Meknes, Morocco to study Arabic this summer at the Arab American Language Institute in Morocco through the CLS program. As a first-generation college student from an underprivileged and low income background, Quinn is incredibly grateful for the opportunities that he has had at PSU and elsewhere.

Kayle Desrosiers - Chinese

Kayle Desrosiers is a junior at PSU, currently working towards a Bachelor of Arts in Liberal Studies with a minor in Russian Language. Kayle has taken a number of language courses at PSU and adores languages as a whole. Kayle has been interested in learning Chinese since 2018 and has occasionally and independently made time to study it.  Nicholas Bruno - Hindi

Nicholas (Nico) Bruno is excited to finally have a travel visa to India and a confirmation of being selected for CLS in Summer 2022.   Still in shock, Nico can't recall ever receiving an award like this before and is glad to share about the experience--particularly if it offers some hope or context for future applicants. Nico came to PSU after attending community college, where he studied Chinese and Spanish and led to his interest in getting a degree in Spanish and Philosophy. In the long run, he hopes to explore cross-cultural religious and philosophical thought through philology and translation.  As a student from a low-income background, he'd also like to help others learn languages, both through teaching and writing.  Nico recommends anyone interested in language (or international relations, because of the nature of the program) to apply each year they can, even if they don't think they'll ever get it.

Melissa LeGree - Persian/Farsi

Melissa will be graduating in Spring 2022 with a degree of Master of Taxation, was selected to study Persian/Farsi through American Councils for International Education Dushanbe Language Center.  She will be involved in an 8-week program of intensive study from June to August, 2021.  She became interested in learning Persian after volunteering with Ascend-Leadership Through Athletics, an international organization with mountaineering programs for young Afghan women.  Melissa intends to pursue a Ph.D. to research historical tax policies and social structures in Afghanistan and plans to use her Spanish and Farsi language skills while working in global development with an international organization.

Melissa encourages future learners of critical languages to take full advantage of the opportunities available as alumni of CLS! To find out more about the Refresh program, go to: https://clscholarship.org/alumni/cls-refresh

Simon Peters - China
