Pre-Professional Tracks

Pre-Education

Pre-Teacher Education is a pre-professional track (not a major) that helps you on the path to becoming a K-12 teacher. Common majors for pre-ed students include arts & letters, social science, general science and liberal studies as well as subject-specific majors for those interested in teaching higher grades.

Pre-Health

Pre-Health is a pre-professional track (not a major) that helps you on the path of pursuing a career in healthcare. PSU offers 14 pre-clinical health pathways, from pre-dentistry to pre-medicine, and Ignite, a leadership and mentorship program.

Pre-Law

Pre-Law is a pre-professional track (not a major) that helps you on the path of pursuing a career in law. Whatever your major, you can add a minor in law and legal studies, which offers an interdisciplinary approach to the study of law with electives in philosophy and history.

Current Student Resources

Find links to the many resources you can access as a student, from academic services and financial aid to counseling and food assistance.

Connect with Advising

Your advisor can help you identify a major that suits you, understand your graduation requirements, create a degree plan and more. Remote and in-person appointments are available.

Race & Ethnic Studies Requirement

The new requirement ensures you're equipped with an understanding of how historically rooted and ongoing racism affects institutions in Oregon, the U.S. and the world. You can double-dip your courses to also fulfill major, minor, University Studies, Honors and/or elective requirements.

University Writing Requirement

You must complete two college-level composition courses or their approved equivalents to graduate.

Second Language Requirement

If you're completing a Bachelor of Arts (B.A.), you must demonstrate proficiency in a second language that is normally attained after two years of college study to graduate.
