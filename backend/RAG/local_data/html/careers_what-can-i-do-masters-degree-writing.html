Government Job Classifications

Classification Specifications are generic descriptions of jobs in government agencies. Generally, they provide an overview of the job and describe essential duties and responsibilities as well as requisite knowledge, skills, and abilities (KSAs) and educational requirements. Classification specifications can be useful in identifying organizations that hire people for jobs that require writing.

City of Portland:

  • Public Information Officer

Multnomah County:

  • Program Communications Specialist
  • Communications Analyst Senior
  • Public Relations Coordinator

City of Beaverton: 

  • Public Information Coordinator

Portland Community College:

Professional Associations

Professional associations, whether they are national, regional, or local, are a great way to network with professionals in your field. These associations may post jobs, organize special interest groups, host conferences, and organize educational, social, or other networking events.

  • American Copy Editors Society (ACES)
  • American Medical Writers Association: American Medical Writers Association Northwest
  • Editorial Freelancers Association - Includes a membership directory
  • IEEE Professional Communication Society
  • Northwest Independent Editors Guild
  • Pacific Northwest Writers Association - Includes links to members websites
  • Society for Technical Communication (STC): Willamette Valley Chapter - Special Interest Group (SIG) for Independent Contractors
  • Willamette Writers
  • Willamette Valley Development Officers - Includes a Membership Directory (available to members) and a Consultant Directory
  • American Association of Advertising Agencies (AAAA)
  • Association for Women in Communications (AWC)
  • National Association of Government Communicators
  • Pacific Northwest Newspapers Association (PNNA)
  • Portland Advertising Federation (PAF)
  • Public Relations Society of America (PRSA)
  • Public Relations Society of America Portland Metro Chapter
