Domestic Student Health Insurance

SUMMER WAIVER STATUS

Summer Only Waiver Status:  Open May 5, 2025 through July 6, 2025 11:59 pm

This is for Summer students charged $917 for insurance and who did not do a waiver in Fall/Winter/Spring of the 2024-25 academic year.  

Access the Summer Only Waiver

Summer Only 2025:  You may do an insurance waiver as long as you have adequate health insurance in place at the beginning of Summer term. The waiver is for domestic students taking 5 or more Summer credits (1 or more if an International student). If you already have an approved waiver on file from Fall 2024 or Winter 2025, or Spring 2025, you do NOT need to do a new waiver until next Fall of 2025.  The Summer waiver deadline is July 6th, 2025.  (The Fall 2025 waiver will go live in August and is for all new and return students)

Please note: If you just registered for 5 or more Summer credits (1 if you are an international student), you must wait 1 business day for the waiver database to be updated in order to access the waiver.

Student Health Insurance Requirement & Eligibility 

Insurance Requirement 

PSU requires that all domestic students enrolled in five credits* or more per term maintain comprehensive health insurance to ensure that students stay healthy and in the classroom. The Student Health Insurance Plan is major medical health insurance providing preventative, routine, and specialized health benefits. Those who have comparable outside health insurance can apply to waive the Student Health Insurance Plan. Students only need to do ONE waiver per academic year which starts each Fall term.

*in-load non-Restricted Differential Tuition credits 

Non-eligible/Restricted Differential Tuition Credits

Some credits, such as those earned during study abroad, the School of Public Health with OHSU, and some continuing education credits are considered Restricted Differential Tuition credits, meaning they do not count toward the 5 credit hours triggering the Student Health Insurance Plan requirement, or count towards eligibility to use SHAC.

Automatic Enrollment

If you are covered by the PSU Student Health Insurance Plan, you will be enrolled automatically and charged $1256 (per term).  You will be charged this rate on your PSU student account each term, as long as you are taking five or more credit hours AND do not submit the health insurance waiver application.

Insurance Plan Eligibility 

Students registered for five or more credits* in a given term are eligible for the PSU Student Health Insurance Plan.

Students who drop below 5 credits* by the end of the second week of the term, will no longer be eligible for the PSU Student Health Insurance Plan for that term.

If a student needs to drop all courses for medical reasons but wishes to continue to have the PSU Student Health Insurance Plan, they may contact the insurance team. 

*in-load non-Restricted Differential Tuition credits

Oregon Health Plan (OHP)

Student With Insurance

Portland State University requires any student taking 5 or more credits per term to maintain comprehensive medical insurance coverage. If a student has their own health insurance, including OHP, they can submit an application to waive the PSU Student Health Insurance Plan.

Student without Insurance

Students who do not have private health insurance by the beginning of the term, will be automatically placed on the PSU Student Health Insurance Plan.

Getting back on the plan

If a PSU Health Insurance Waiver is approved during the current academic year, but you later lose your health insurance coverage, you may choose to opt back in to the Student Health Insurance Plan within 30 days of the loss of coverage by contacting the SHAC Insurance Team. 

NonCompliant/Failed Audit

If a student submits an application to waive the Student Health Insurance Plan and is not approved, OR received notification from SHAC that their waiver failed a routine insurance audit, fill out a Request for Consideration Form.
10 business days from denied waiver/failed to appeal.

Oregon Health Plan (OHP)

Students covered by the Oregon Health Plan (OHP) or Medicaid (Health Share, Care Oregon, Washington Apple, etc.) meet the University's requirements to waive the Student Health Insurance Plan. If you are not sure if your private insurance plan meets PSU's waiver criteria, contact us.
